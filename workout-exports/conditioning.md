## GEN-COND-BURPEE — Engine builder — burpee density

Category: conditioning | Type: conditioning | Variant: burpee
Difficulty: intermediate | Duration: 50 min
Equipment: rower, running
Focus: Work capacity, Burpees
Tags: conditioning, burpees, engine, build_phase, intermediate

Description: Burpee-focused engine work

Progression: Burpee density: hold cycle time round 1 vs 4 within 3 sec before adding reps or removing rest.

RPE: Sustainable metcon RPE 7–8; peak pieces may touch RPE 9 on the last round only.

Coaching cues:
  - Hands under shoulders; chest and thighs touch on the floor.
  - Stand up fully before the jump — no half reps under fatigue.
  - Step-back or box burpees are race-legal when grip and lungs are gone.
  - Find a cadence you can hold for 80 reps — sprinting the first 20 costs the run.

Substitutions: Match the written effort, rest, and rep scheme. Substitutions are for missing equipment — not to make the session easier.

Step-back burpees or box burpees count — keep full range of motion and the same rep total.

In AMRAPs/EMOMs, swap stations but keep work/rest intervals identical.

--- SESSION ---

Warm-up:
6 min row easy + dynamic prep

Circuit:
5 rounds: 10 burpee broad jumps, 12 cal row, 200m run

Finisher:
8 min AMRAP: 8 KB swings + 8 box step-ups

Cool-down:
5 min walk + breathing
